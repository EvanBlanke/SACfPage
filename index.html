Get the most out of your sunrise

A few small choices make a big difference in how natural your wake-up feels.

🛏️

Put a light where you can see it

A bedside lamp or a light within your line of sight works best. Light reaching your eyes — even through closed lids — is what signals your body to wake.

⏳

Start with a 20–30 minute sunrise

A longer fade feels more like real dawn. If you're a deep sleeper, try a longer duration and a brighter finish; light sleepers may prefer 10–15 minutes.

🎨

Use warm colors if your bulbs support them

Real sunrises begin deep red-orange and shift toward bright daylight. Color-capable bulbs make the effect dramatically more convincing.

📅

Keep a consistent wake time

Your circadian rhythm loves routine. Waking at the same time daily — weekends included — makes the sunrise effect stronger over time.

📶

Keep your HomeKit setup responsive

A home hub (HomePod or Apple TV) and a solid Wi-Fi signal near your bedroom help your lights respond reliably, right on schedule.

Frequently asked questions

Which smart lights work with Sunrise Alarm?

Any light that works with Apple HomeKit. If you can control it from Apple's Home app — Philips Hue, Nanoleaf, LIFX, Eve, Meross, and many others — Sunrise Alarm can use it for your wake-up sunrise.

Is this really a one-time purchase?

Yes. You pay once on the App Store and own the app forever, with all features included. There is no subscription, no trial that converts, and no in-app purchases to unlock anything.

Is it available on Android?

No — Sunrise Alarm is built exclusively for iOS and Apple HomeKit. Being iOS-only lets us integrate deeply with HomeKit and keep everything on-device.

What data does the app collect?

None. We do not collect, store, or share any personal information. Your alarms and settings stay on your device or within Apple's HomeKit ecosystem. Read the full privacy policy.

Do I need a HomePod or Apple TV?

A home hub (HomePod, HomePod mini, or Apple TV) is Apple's recommended setup for reliable HomeKit automation and is the best way to make sure your sunrise starts on time, every time.

Will the sunrise wake me without sound?

Many people find light alone is enough once they adjust, but you can keep an audible alarm as a backup. The sunrise does its job before the alarm rings — most mornings you'll wake before you ever hear it.
